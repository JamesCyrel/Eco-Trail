North Highway District Eco-Trail Tour Package

An Immersive 3-Day Eco-Tour Experience

Welcome to the North Highway District Eco-Trail, an immersive 3-day eco-tour designed for those who seek to connect with the natural beauty, rich culture, and unique man-made landmarks of Malaybalay City, Bukidnon. From breathtaking waterfalls and local traditions to enjoying the comfort and charm of local resorts, the Eco-Trail offers a customized adventure like no other.

Choose from Natural, Cultural, or Man-made attractions each day, and build the perfect experience tailored to your interests. With a focus on sustainability, local community engagement, and memorable experiences, the Eco-Trail is your gateway to discovering the hidden gems of the North Highway District.

Day 1

Water & Forest Awakening (Natural Attractions)

Immerse yourself in natural attractions

Morning Activities

Trek to Kinapuntan Falls

Location: Kinapuntan Falls, tucked within the Manalog forests of Bukidnon and celebrated for its cliffside drop and cooling mist.

Accessibility: Expect a moderate trek along forest trails with shallow river crossings and uneven, rocky segments. Support from eco-guides is provided, and sturdy hiking shoes are highly recommended.

Transportation: 4x4 vehicles ferry the group to the jump-off point since the rugged approach trail is inaccessible to standard vehicles.

Biodiversity Learning Session

Location: Tagoloan Headwaters.

Accessibility: This area is easily accessible by vehicles. There will be some walking along designated trails to explore the local ecosystem.

Transportation: Guests will be transported by eco-van to this site.

Freebies for Day 1

  • Free 4x4 transport to the Kinapuntan Falls jump-off
  • Free Eco-Gear Rental: Trekking poles and biodegradable bags to help you enjoy the tour sustainably

Afternoon Activities

Optional Eco-Camp Experience

Location: Eco-camp near the river or in the forest, offering breathtaking natural views.

Accessibility: Camps are generally a short to moderate walk from the drop-off point. Trails can be moderately steep but manageable for most visitors, with guides available to assist.

Transportation: Transportation to the camp is provided via 4x4 vehicles, as access roads are rough and require sturdy vehicles.

Inclusions

Eco-friendly gear rental (trekking poles, biodegradable bags) Light, organic meals sourced from local ingredients Educational session on river ecology and conservation

Sustainability Focus

All activities follow Leave-No-Trace principles. Biodegradable materials only, with emphasis on water conservation and ecosystem protection.

Day 2

Culture, Farms & Everyday Highlands (Cultural Attractions)

Experience cultural attractions and local heritage

Morning Activities

Local Community Storytelling

Location: Kalasungay or Can-ayan.

Accessibility: These communities are easily accessible by standard vehicles, but some of the villages are located on uphill roads. A short walk (approximately 5-10 minutes) may be needed to reach the community center where the storytelling will take place.

Transportation: Guests will be transported via eco-van from the hotel to the community center.

Craft Demonstrations

Location: Local workshops in Kalasungay.

Accessibility: The craft workshops are located in open areas with easy access for all visitors. Some areas may have a slight incline but are generally accessible for most people.

Transportation: Guests will continue the journey via eco-van.

Freebies for Day 2

  • Free Local Souvenir: A handcrafted item made by local artisans
  • Free Transport for the farm visit to avoid any additional walking

Afternoon Activities

Farm Visit

Location: Imbayao or Can-ayan

Accessibility:The farm visit involves a short walk through farming areas, which may become muddy after rain. Some terrain is uneven, so the tour may not be fully accessible for guests with mobility limitations. Comfortable shoes and light walking are recommended.

Transportation: Guests will be dropped off at the farm entrance by eco-van. Farm staff will guide the walking tour and explain the farm’s operations, crops, and practices.

Community Café Dinner

Location: A local café in Patpat or nearby village.

Accessibility: The café is accessible by standard vehicle, and the location is fully wheelchair-accessible.

Transportation: Eco-van will transport guests to the café for dinner.

Inclusions

Cultural souvenirs (handcrafted jewelry or traditional items) Lunch and dinner featuring locally sourced ingredients Immersive cultural experience with local guides and performers

Community Benefits

Direct support to local artisans and farmers through revenue-sharing models. Authentic cultural experiences led by community members.

Day 3

Ridges, Cafés & Sky Serenity (Man-made Attractions)

Explore man-made attractions and scenic views

Morning Activities

Resort

Location: Can‑ayan, Sumpong, Kalasungay, Malaybalay City.

Accessibility: These resorts feature mostly level paths and walkable areas, though some sections may require light walking. Certain areas may not be fully accessible for guests with mobility limitations.

Transportation: Guests will be transported by eco‑van if roads are passable; alternative arrangements may be required if road conditions are rough or muddy.

Freebies for Day 3

  • Free Transport to all resort and café hopping locations
  • Free Drink at One Café of the guest's choice (Coffee, Tea, or Fresh Juice)

Afternoon Activities

Café Hopping

Location: Local cafés such as Fortune Valley Café, Bean Zone, and Kiamo Ridge Café

Accessibility: These cafés are located along the road and are accessible by standard vehicles. No walking required between cafés unless guests wish to explore the surrounding areas.

Transportation: Guests will be transported by eco-van to visit each café.

Sunset Viewing

Location: Can-ayan and Imbayao

Accessibility: The visit involves a short walk from the vehicle drop-off points to the viewing decks. The terrain is mostly smooth with slight inclines and is generally well-maintained. The areas may not be fully accessible for guests with mobility limitations. Comfortable shoes are recommended.

Transportation: Guests will be dropped off at the viewing areas by eco-van or standard vehicle. Guided visits may be provided to highlight the panoramic views and surrounding scenery.

Inclusions

Solar-powered charging stations and eco-friendly amenities Meals and drinks at eco-conscious cafés Guided reflection session on conservation and sustainability

Sustainability Focus

Solar-powered facilities at all stops. Optional carbon-offset donations supporting local environmental projects.
